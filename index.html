I found this article very interesting in the discussion of Western/ Eurocentric design.

White supremacy in design is something that has come up a few times during my time at MICA.

I found the analogies and examples from this article extremely helpful in contextualizing this conversation.

The discussion of how Helvetica was intended to be completely neutral was most interesting to me.

I agree that given that Helvetica was created with the Western, white idea of universality inherently makes it non-neutral.
I also found the example of the font “chop suey” on take out boxes most helpful in understanding how appropriated design can be when white western influence attempts to include “others.” Finally I found the discussion of the paradox of “white culture” individualistic yet invisible- and how it relates to the modernism movement very interesting, as it was a point I’d never considered but holds a lot of truth.
